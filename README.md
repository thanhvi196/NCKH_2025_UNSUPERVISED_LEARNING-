# NCKH_2025_UNSUPERVISED_LEARNING
## UNSUPERVISED LEARNING AND CREDIT RATING: A CLUSTERING STUDY OF VIETNAM

This study develops a transparent, theory-informed segmentation of Vietnamese listed firms for credit-rating purposes by applying K means clustering to a multi-dimensional set of fundamentals, valuation, capital structure, ownership, and location variables. The dataset comprises 3,528 firm-year observations for 692 firms on the Ho Chi Minh Stock Exchange and the Hanoi Stock Exchange during 2019 to 2024, drawn from Refinitiv. The data are cleaned by removing missing and inconsistent records and filtering extreme observations using a Z-score rule before standardizing continuous features. The number of clusters is chosen using internal validation criteria, including the silhouette coefficient, the Calinski–Harabasz index, and the Davies–Bouldin index, yielding a four-cluster solution that balances compactness and separation and remains stable across random initializations. The largest cluster combines lower leverage, higher foreign ownership, and stronger valuation signals; a second cluster is more debt dependent; a third displays tight short-term liquidity; and a small residual cluster exhibits very high profitability with very low leverage. Principal component analysis confirms visual separation, and cross-tabulations by sector and region show that the structure is not driven by a single industry or location. The results provide an interpretable, reproducible map of firm heterogeneity that can support screening, monitoring, and pricing decisions in a credit-rating orientation.
